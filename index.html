01 / 07 — THE MORSE LANDSCAPE

Starting Point

A function encodes topology

Let $M$ be a compact smooth manifold and $f : M \to \mathbb{R}$ a smooth function whose critical points are all non-degenerate, meaning the Hessian $\mathrm{Hess}_p\, f$ is invertible at every critical point $p$. Such a function is called a Morse function.

Definition

The Morse index $\lambda = \mathrm{ind}(p)$ is the number of negative eigenvalues of $\mathrm{Hess}_p\, f$. Near $p$, the Morse Lemma gives coordinates in which $$f = f(p) - x_1^2 - \cdots - x_\lambda^2 + x_{\lambda+1}^2 + \cdots + x_n^2.$$

Equip $M$ with a Riemannian metric $g$. The negative gradient flow $\dot x = -\nabla f(x)$ is the dynamical system that descends $f$ as steeply as possible. Since $\frac{d}{dt}f(\phi_t(x)) = -\|\nabla f\|^2 \leq 0$, the function $f$ is a Lyapunov function: every trajectory flows strictly downhill toward critical points.

Compactness

When trajectories break

In the Morse-Smale setting, the moduli space of flow lines from $p$ to $q$ (modulo time reparametrisation), $$\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(p,q) = W^u(p) \cap W^s(q)\, /\, \mathbb{R},$$ is a smooth manifold of dimension $\mathrm{ind}(p) - \mathrm{ind}(q) - 1$.

Compactification Theorem

The closure $\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(p,q)}$ is compact, and every boundary point is a broken trajectory: $$\partial\,\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(p,q)} = \bigsqcup_{r}\,\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(p,r)\times\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(r,q)$$ where $r$ ranges over intermediate critical points.

Geometrically: a trajectory from $p$ to $q$ can spend increasingly long time near an intermediate critical point $r$. In the limit, the single trajectory breaks into two pieces, each a genuine flow line. The Morse index strictly decreases at each break, so the chain is finite.

This compactification is not pathological: it is conical. The neighbourhood of a broken point looks like a product of trajectory spaces crossed with a cone parameter measuring the breaking time.

03 / 07 — STRATIFIED CLOSURES

Main Result

The closure is a stratified space

The closure $\overline{W^s(p)}$ decomposes into smooth pieces indexed by critical points of lower index: $$\overline{W^s(p)} = \bigsqcup_{q\,:\,\mathrm{ind}(q)\leq\mathrm{ind}(p)} S_q, \quad S_q = W^s(q)\cap\overline{W^s(p)}.$$

This is a genuine Whitney stratification: the strata are smooth manifolds, they satisfy the frontier condition ($S_r \cap \overline{S_q} \neq \emptyset \Rightarrow S_r \subset \overline{S_q}$), and they satisfy Whitney's condition (B): tangent spaces of higher strata cannot tilt away from the direction of approach from lower strata.

Main Theorem (unified proof)

The stratification $\{S_q\}$ of $\overline{W^s(p)}$ is simultaneously locally conical (Laudenbach) and Whitney regular (Whitney-Mather). These two descriptions are equivalent in the Morse-Smale setting.

The conical model near $x \in S_q$: $$U \cap \overline{W^s(p)}\; \cong\; (S_q \cap U) \times C(L_x)$$ where $L_x \cong \bigsqcup \widehat{\mathcal{M}}(q,r_1)\times\cdots\times\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(r_k,p)$ is a product of moduli spaces — the link at $x$.

04 / 07 — THE CONCEPTUAL BRIDGE

Key Observation

CT reconstruction is a gradient flow

X-ray Computed Tomography measures line integrals of an attenuation function $\mu : \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ via the Radon transform: $g = \mathcal{R}\mu + \varepsilon.$ Reconstruction inverts this: find $\mu$ from $g$.

Modern reconstruction methods, whether iterative, variational, or neural, minimise an energy functional: $$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \|\mathcal{R}f_\theta - g\|^2 + \lambda R(f_\theta)$$ where $f_\theta$ is the reconstructed image (parametrised by $\theta$) and $R$ is a regulariser. Training proceeds by gradient descent: $$\dot\theta(t) = -\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}(\theta(t)).$$

Left: gradient flow on a Morse landscape (abstract). Right: gradient descent on a CT reconstruction loss surface (concrete). The mathematical structure is identical.

Morse Theory Side

— Parameter space: compact manifold $M$
— Energy: Morse function $f : M \to \mathbb{R}$
— Dynamics: $\dot x = -\nabla f(x)$
— Critical points: hyperbolic, finitely many
— Invariant sets: $W^s(p)$, $W^u(p)$ smooth
— Closures: Whitney stratified, conical

Neural Reconstruction Side

— Parameter space: weight manifold $\Theta \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N$
— Energy: loss $\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \|\mathcal{R}f_\theta - g\|^2 + \lambda R$
— Dynamics: SGD / Adam / L-BFGS
— Critical points: local minima, saddles, …
— Invariant sets: basins of attraction
— Closures: structure unknown (open problem)

The left column is well-understood. The right column is where the research lives. The central question is: how much of the left column's geometric structure survives in the right column?

05 / 07 — IMPLICIT NEURAL REPRESENTATIONS

The Representation

A network as a continuous image

An Implicit Neural Representation (INR) replaces a discrete voxel grid with a neural network $f_\theta : \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ that maps spatial coordinates to attenuation values. The image is stored in the weights $\theta$, not in a grid.

Advantages: continuous (query at any resolution), compact (millions of parameters instead of billions of voxels), differentiable (backpropagation through $f_\theta$ and the CT projector gives gradients with respect to $\theta$).

SIREN Architecture

$f_\theta(x) = W_n(\sin(W_{n-1}(\cdots \sin(W_1 x + b_1)\cdots) + b_{n-1})) + b_n$

Sinusoidal activations give exact gradients at all frequencies. Crucial for representing sharp features like bone-tissue boundaries in CT.

The optimisation problem becomes: given CT measurements $g$, find $$\theta^* = \arg\min_\theta \|\mathcal{R}f_\theta - g\|^2 + \lambda R(f_\theta).$$ This is a gradient flow on the weight manifold $\Theta$.

06 / 07 — THE OPEN QUESTION

Research Direction

Does the loss landscape stratify?

In the Morse-Smale setting, the loss landscape is well-understood: finitely many critical points, smooth invariant manifolds, Whitney-regular closures, conical local models. This is the content of the main theorem.

In the INR-CT setting, the loss landscape is far more complex: the parameter space $\Theta$ is high-dimensional, the critical structure is degenerate (the Morse-Smale condition likely fails), and the landscape depends on the specific architecture, the measurement operator, and the regulariser.

Open Question

Let $\mathcal{L} : \Theta \to \mathbb{R}$ be the reconstruction loss of an INR model for CT. Under what conditions on the architecture $f_\theta$ and the measurement operator $\mathcal{R}$ does the gradient flow $\dot\theta = -\nabla_\theta\mathcal{L}(\theta)$ generate a stratified decomposition of $\Theta$ with locally conical models?

More precisely: do the basins of attraction of local minima of $\mathcal{L}$ have closures that admit Whitney-regular stratifications? And if so, does the Laudenbach–Whitney-Mather equivalence proved for Morse-Smale systems extend to this non-generic, infinite-resolution setting?

A positive answer would have concrete consequences. It would explain:

  • why coarse-to-fine optimisation works: it follows connecting trajectories between strata of the landscape hierarchy;
  • why certain initialisations reliably avoid bad local minima: they begin in specific strata whose closures do not intersect the global minimum's basin boundary;
  • how to design regularisers that enforce desired geometric properties of the loss landscape, rather than relying on empirical tuning.
Conjecture (informal)

For SIREN-type INRs with Tikhonov regularisation and the Radon forward operator, the loss landscape $\mathcal{L}$ is a Morse-Bott function on a stratified subset of $\Theta$, and the stable manifold closures of its critical submanifolds admit conical models whose links are products of spaces of connecting trajectories. A direct generalisation of the finite-dimensional Morse-Smale picture.

Making this conjecture precise, i.e. identifying the right regularity class, the right notion of Morse-Bott in this setting, and the correct generalisation of the Laudenbach conical construction to degenerate and infinite-dimensional analogues, could be the mathematical programme of this project.
